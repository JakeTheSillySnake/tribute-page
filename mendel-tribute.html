Gregor Johann Mendel

The man who discovered the laws of inheritance

Here are the events of Gregor Mendel's life:

  • 1822 - Born in Heinzendorf bei Odrau, Austrian Empire (now Czechia)
  • 1840-1843 - Studied philosophy and physics at the Philosophical Institute of the University of Olomouc
  • 1850 - Failed oral part of an exam to become a certified high school teacher
  • 1851- Sent to the University of Vienna to study under the sponsorship of Abbot Cyril Franti≈°ek Napp
  • 1853 - Returned to the Augustinian St Thomas's Abbey in Brno as a teacher
  • 1856-1863 - Cultivated and tested ~28,000 plants, mainly pea plants. His experiments led to the Law of Segregation and the Law of Independent Assortment, now known as Mendel's Laws of Inheritance
  • 1865 - Presented his paper "Experiments on Plant Hybridization" at two meetings of the Natural History Society of Brno, which was positively accepted but produced no impact
  • 1866 - Mendel's paper published but was seen as being about hybridization rather than inheritance and, again, received little acknowledgment
  • 1867 - Replaced Napp as abbot of the monastery
  • 1884 - Died at the age of 61 in Brno from chronic nephritis
  • 1900 - Mendel's work was independently rediscovered by Hugo de Vries and Carl Correns and the results were replicated
  • 1930s-1940s - Mendel's laws were combined with the Darwin-Wallace theory of natural selection, producing modern synthesis of evolutionary biology
